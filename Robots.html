•What does it do?

Just have a look around you, you will find robots everywhere in your life whether it’s a simple robot like vacuum cleaner or more complex robots which work in manufacturing industries. In fact we can say that robots are one of the best inventions that humans and science have produced. You can get anything done by a robot.

As per the current technology, robots are categorised into 6 categories: -

1)Pre-programmed robots: They are operational in a controlled environment and are useful for doing a monotonous task such as welding or inserting a part in an engine. These kinds of robots are mostly used in industries.

Pre-programmed robots are further divided into: (i)Cartesian Robots, (ii)Articulated Robots, (iii)SCARA Robots, (iv)Delta Robots

(i) Cartesian Robots: The last one is Cartesian robots. They are mostly configured to have 3 or more linear actuators and brackets making it customisable according to its application and at the same time being cost effective by reducing complexity. Articulated robots can be attached to it to increase capabilities. A drawback of this type of robot is they can not work in dirty or dusty environments due to its exposed hinges and sliding parts.

(ii)Articulated Robots: The most commonly used robot is Articulated Robot. This is the type of robot which most people have perceptionised. It is much like a CNC machine. They are further classified by the number of axes or joints they have. The most common one is a 6 axis robot. These types of robots are generally used for industrial and commercial purposes such as welding, joining or cleaning ceilings or doing certain things with precision.

(iii)SCARA Robots: The next one is SCARA robot which means Selective Compliance Articulated Robot Arm. They are operational between two parallel planes and can do work like transferring the objects from one conveyor belt to another or inserting a pin in a vertical direction without binding it. They are lightweight, cost effective and efficient.But due to their limited arm length and fixed orientation they have certain limitations.

(iv)Delta Robots: The other one is Delta robots. They are also referred to as spider bots. It uses three base-mounted motors to actuate control arms that position the wrist. Unlike articulated robots, an actuator is mounted on a stationary wrist making it very light weight. This allows very swift movements where high speed operations are required.

Humanoid Robots: These robots look alike and are expected to do tasks which are done by humans such as running, walking etc. Also, they are given expressions and a human-like face. Examples of these robots are Asimo by Honda.

2)Autonomous Robots: They can be operated independently of human operators.They are programmed to carry out tasks in an open environment without human supervision. Example is automatic vacuum cleaner by xiaomi.

3)Teleoperated Robots: These are mechanical bots controlled by humans. They usually work in extreme geographical and environmental conditions. Example is underwater submarines used to fix pipelines or drones.

4)Augmenting robot: They are designed to enhance current human capabilities or replace which are lost. Example is a false motorised limb or an exoskeleton used to lift heavy objects.

Robots will also be seen in defense, they will be accompanying the military as smart weapons and can be proved to be a fatal weapon for opponents. At the same, robots will be competing with humans, which will negatively affect some of the jobs. According to a study by CNBC robots would take over 20 million jobs by 2030.
